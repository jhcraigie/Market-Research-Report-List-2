<p><p>Medical bioresorbable materials are categorized into different types such as PLA (polylactic acid), PGA (polyglycolic acid), PCL (polycaprolactone), polysaccharides, and others. PLA is widely used due to its biocompatibility and mechanical properties. PGA is known for its fast degradation rate. PCL offers flexibility and slow degradation. Polysaccharides are natural and biodegradable materials derived from sugars. Other bioresorbable materials include calcium phosphates, silk proteins, and collagen. Each type has its unique properties and applications in the medical field.</p></p>
