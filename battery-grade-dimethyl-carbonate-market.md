<p><p>Battery Grade Dimethyl Carbonate can be produced using two main methods: Propylene Oxide Method and Ethylene Oxide Method. The Propylene Oxide Method involves the reaction of propylene oxide with carbon dioxide to produce dimethyl carbonate. On the other hand, the Ethylene Oxide Method involves the reaction of ethylene oxide with carbon dioxide to produce dimethyl carbonate. Both methods result in high-quality dimethyl carbonate suitable for use in batteries, providing a cleaner and more sustainable alternative to traditional battery components.</p></p>
