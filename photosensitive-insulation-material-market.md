<p><p>Photosensitive insulation materials are divided into two main types: organic and inorganic. Organic photosensitive insulation materials are derived from natural sources such as plants and animals, and are often biodegradable. In contrast, inorganic photosensitive insulation materials are manufactured from chemical compounds such as silicones and ceramics, and are known for their high thermal stability and durability. Both types of materials offer unique properties and are used in various applications, ranging from electronic components to construction materials.</p></p>
