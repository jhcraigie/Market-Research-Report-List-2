<p><p>Automotive constant velocity joint market can be categorized into two types based on their placement in a vehicle's drivetrain system - outboard joints and inboard joints. Outboard joints are located at the ends of the drive shafts, connecting them to the wheels, while inboard joints are positioned closer to the transmission, connecting the drive shafts to the differential. Both types of joints play a crucial role in maintaining smooth power transfer and allowing for varying angles of rotation in the drivetrain system of a vehicle.</p></p>
