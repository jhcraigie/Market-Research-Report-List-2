<p><p>GNSS chips are categorized into two main types: High Precision GNSS Chips and Standard Precision GNSS Chips. High Precision GNSS Chips are designed for applications that require highly accurate positioning, such as in surveying, mapping, and agriculture. These chips offer centimeter-level accuracy and are more expensive than standard precision chips. Standard Precision GNSS Chips, on the other hand, provide accuracy within a few meters and are commonly used in consumer devices like smartphones and navigation systems. Both types play a crucial role in enabling location-based services and navigation technology.</p></p>
