<p><p>Wireless power controller ICs are divided into two main types: Transmitter ICs and Receiver ICs. Transmitter ICs are responsible for converting power from a source into a wireless power signal, which is then transmitted to the receiver. Receiver ICs, on the other hand, are designed to capture the wireless power signal and convert it back into usable electricity for charging devices. Both types play a crucial role in enabling wireless charging technology to function effectively in various applications.</p></p>
