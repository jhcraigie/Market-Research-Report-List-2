<p><p>Non-linear optocouplers are available in various types based on their output configurations. Photovoltaic-output optocouplers use light sensors to generate electrical signals. Transistor-output optocouplers use a transistor to amplify and switch the signal. Triac-output and thyristor-output optocouplers use AC switches for controlling power. IC-output optocouplers integrate amplifiers and logic circuits for complex applications. Other types of optocouplers may include digital outputs or specialty configurations for specific industry needs. Each type offers unique capabilities for signal isolation and communication in various electronic systems.</p></p>
