<p><p>Hybrid vehicles come in three main market types: series, parallel, and series-parallel. In series hybrid vehicles, the internal combustion engine serves only as a generator to charge the battery, while an electric motor provides the propulsion. Parallel hybrids use both the engine and electric motor to power the vehicle simultaneously. Series-parallel hybrids combine elements of both series and parallel systems to optimize efficiency and performance. Each market type offers unique benefits and cater to different driving needs.</p></p>
