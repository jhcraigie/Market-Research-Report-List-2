<p><p>Geosynthetic Clay Liner (GCL) market types are determined by various factors such as Bentonite Swell Index, Fluid Loss, Mass/Area, Grab Strength, Peel Strength, Index Flux, Hydraulic Conductivity, and Hydrated Internal Shear Strength. Bentonite Swell Index indicates the ability of the liner to expand when in contact with water. Fluid Loss measures the amount of liquid lost when pressure is applied. Mass/Area determines the density of the liner. Grab Strength and Peel Strength reflect the liner's resistance to tearing. Index Flux and Hydraulic Conductivity measure the liner's permeability. Hydrated Internal Shear Strength indicates the liner's stability under stress.</p></p>
